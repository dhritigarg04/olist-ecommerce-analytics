01 · Problem Statement

What Business Question Are We Solving?

Olist, a Brazilian marketplace aggregator, connects thousands of small retailers to major e-commerce platforms. With rapid market expansion between 2016–2018, the leadership team needs to understand: Which categories, cities, and customer segments are driving growth — and where are the operational and revenue leakage risks that need addressing?

This analysis transforms 9 relational tables into a unified analytical layer, then extracts KPIs, trend narratives, and strategic recommendations — structured as an executive-ready case study.

Specifically, this project investigates:

  • Revenue and order volume momentum across 20 months of trading
  • Geographic concentration risk in the customer base
  • Product category mix and monetisation efficiency
  • Delivery performance vs. customer expectations
  • Payment behaviour and average order value evolution

03 · Chart-by-Chart Insights

What the Visualisations Reveal

1 Monthly Revenue Trend

Revenue grew from under R$500K/month in early 2017 to a peak of over R$1.2M in November 2017 — a 140% spike aligned with Black Friday. The growth trajectory then stabilised into 2018, suggesting market maturation rather than continued hypergrowth. This pattern mirrors classic e-commerce S-curve adoption in emerging markets.

2 Monthly Order Volume

Order counts track closely with revenue, but the AOV (Chart 9) rising simultaneously tells a richer story — Olist's customer base is not just growing in size, they are spending more per visit. The November 2017 spike is even more pronounced in order volume, confirming Black Friday as the platform's single biggest demand event.

3 Top Cities by Customer Base

São Paulo alone accounts for over 15,000+ unique customers — more than the next 4 cities combined. Rio de Janeiro and Belo Horizonte are distant second and third. This severe geographic concentration in the Southeast corridor exposes Olist to regional demand risk and signals a large, underserved market in Brazil's interior states.

4 Top Product Categories

Bed, Bath & Table and Health & Beauty lead in sales volume, indicating that everyday lifestyle goods — not electronics — are driving marketplace throughput. This differs from global e-commerce norms where electronics dominate, and suggests Olist's value proposition resonates most in home and personal care verticals.

5 Top Customers by Revenue

The top 10 customers contribute disproportionately high lifetime values. The distribution suggests the presence of B2B buyers or power users placing high-value repeat orders — a segment that warrants dedicated retention and loyalty investment, potentially through a VIP or wholesale programme.

6 Payment Value Distribution

The distribution is right-skewed, with a median around R$100–120 but a long tail extending to R$3,000+. This skew means the mean overestimates typical spend — a critical nuance for inventory planning and pricing strategy. The bulk of transactions cluster in the R$50–300 range, defining the true "sweet spot" of the marketplace.

7 Delivery Delay Analysis

Approximately 7–8% of orders arrive late versus the promised date — a relatively strong performance for a marketplace spanning Brazil's vast geography. However, late deliveries correlate with lower review scores (visible in Chart 10 cross-analysis), making this a direct customer satisfaction lever. Early deliveries are rewarded with 4.5+ ratings on average.

8 Order Status Distribution

"Delivered" accounts for over 96% of all orders — a strong fulfillment health signal. However, the ~625 cancelled orders and ~609 unavailable orders represent recoverable revenue. Investigating the cancellation drivers (stock-outs, price issues, customer indecision) could unlock a meaningful conversion improvement.

9 Average Order Value Trend

AOV climbs from approximately R$140 in early 2017 to over R$175 by mid-2018 — a ~25% increase — suggesting that either higher-value product categories are gaining share, or customers are adding more items per basket over time. This is a key indicator of marketplace maturation and customer trust development.

10 Review Score Distribution

A striking bimodal distribution: customers either rate Olist 5-stars (~57%) or 1-star (~11%), with limited middle-ground scoring. This polarisation signals that experiences are rarely "okay" — they are either delightful or deeply disappointing. The operational trigger for negative reviews is almost always delivery failure or product misrepresentation.

04 · Business Insights

Strategic Intelligence from the Data

👤

Customer Behaviour Insights

  • The customer base is almost entirely first-time buyers — repeat purchase rates appear very low, a known structural challenge in Brazilian e-commerce. Retention programmes are a high-ROI opportunity.
  • Geographic concentration in São Paulo (>15% of customers) creates resilience risk if regional economic conditions shift. Diversification into Tier-2 cities could reduce this dependency.
  • The bimodal review pattern suggests experience quality is highly variable across sellers, not uniformly poor — pointing to a seller quality management problem rather than a platform design issue.
📈

Sales & Revenue Insights

  • The November 2017 Black Friday event generated the single largest revenue month — but growth did not meaningfully accelerate post-event. This suggests the event attracted deal-seekers rather than converting loyal repeat customers.
  • The top 3 product categories (Bed/Bath/Table, Health/Beauty, Sports/Leisure) collectively account for ~30%+ of volume — a healthy diversification compared to single-category-dominated platforms.
  • Rising AOV (+25% over 18 months) is a strong monetisation signal. If sustained, it implies each incremental customer is worth more than the cohort before — a positive unit economics story.
🚚

Operational & Logistics Insights

  • With ~93% on-time delivery, Olist outperforms many comparable emerging-market marketplaces. However, the 7% late delivery rate disproportionately drives negative reviews, making last-mile reliability the single biggest lever for NPS improvement.
  • Freight costs embedded in order items suggest significant regional freight disparities — sellers in the interior face higher logistics costs, which may be structurally limiting their price competitiveness vs. São Paulo-based sellers.
  • Order processing gaps (approved_at delays vs. purchase_timestamp) suggest bottlenecks in payment gateway confirmation, which could be adding friction and increasing cart abandonment.
05 · Strategic Recommendations

Four Priority Actions for Growth

🎯 01 · Launch a Retention Engine

With near-zero repeat purchase rates, a structured post-purchase CRM programme (personalised re-engagement emails, loyalty credit on 2nd order) could increase LTV by 40–60% based on comparable marketplace benchmarks. Target: top 20% of spenders first.

🗺️ 02 · Geographic Expansion Strategy

Curitiba, Fortaleza, Manaus, and Recife show demand indicators but low seller density. A targeted seller acquisition programme in these cities would reduce freight times, cut delivery costs, and unlock a ~25M person addressable customer base currently underserved.

📦 03 · Seller Quality Tiering

Institute a transparent seller quality scoring system (delivery SLA, review score, cancellation rate) with tiered benefits — better search placement for top-tier sellers, remediation support for at-risk sellers. This would shift the review distribution away from the polarised 1-star/5-star pattern.

💳 04 · AOV Growth via Bundling

The R$100–300 purchase sweet spot suggests customers are price-conscious but not bargain-only buyers. Cross-category bundling recommendations (e.g., Home + Personal Care) and instalment payment promotion (Brazil's strong "parcelamento" culture) could push AOV from R$175 toward R$220+ without acquiring new customers.
